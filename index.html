Waste not, want not: Wandsworth

Previous Next

Every year in the UK, 1.9 million tonnes of food is wasted. We need to waste less.

But some food waste in inevitable. Tea bags, vegetable peelings etc need to go somewhere.

So what can we do to limit the impact our rubbish is having?

  1. Think about the packaging of your food. Can you buy fruit and veg loose instead of wrapped in plastic?
  2. Keep track of what's in your fridge! Around 1m tonnes of food is binned whole or in unopened packaging, usually because it's gone past its sell-by date.
  3. For things you do need to throw away, use a food waste bin rather than throwing everything in landfill together.

But you can only do that if you have a food waste bin!

Most boroughs in London collect food waste, but Wandsworth is one of those that don't.

Wandsworth is also the sixth-worst borough in the UK for household waste recycling. Coincidence?
